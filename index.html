The formulation of gameplay shapes a game's strategies in a large way, making some units more effective in certain situations or against other units. Most Turn Based Strategy games have a limited selection of units, which has a significant impact on both how the game is played and how fun it is. Opening up unit design to the user rather than only a preselected set of units will allow for users to design armies which truly fit their gameplay style. From lone heroes with thick armor and heavy weapons to gigantic hordes of minor peons who can barely hold up their weapons (and everything in between), all styles are playable.

Adjust your army in hundreds of ways! With different base unit groups like Robots, Humans, Night Elves, and Orcs, which all have special attributes common to some or all of the many base units available to the race, you can adjust the balance between the number of rangers, war machines, infantry, and cavalry units. Or you can take away one unit's upgrade to give an upgrade to a different unit. Battles take place with a specified point cap and any army which fits inside this cap is legal, so there will be countless variations to try and to be prepared to fight against!

FAQ

What type of game is ZollSpace?
Zollspace is a turn based strategy game where you control an army of units entirely created and customized by YOU!
Who will I be fighting?
The computer or other players, your choice. There will be a campaign version, random maps, and rated PvP action as well, so there will always be playable content.
Rated PvP? What is that?
Players who fight against each other will receive ratings, which change over time, going up after a win and down after a loss. This lets people know exactly where their skill is relative to other players. Beating a higher rated opponent will result in more of a ratings change than beating a lower rated opponent.
What is Unit Score?
Unit Score is how much an individual unit is worth, relative to other units. This is how the game is balanced; 100 points worth of buffed up hero should win against 100 points of weak peons approximately 50% of the time, barring any strategic advantages. This allows for ANY army configuration to be playable, and makes strategy count for more than unit selection.
What is the Battle Points Cap?
Battles are classed by how many points worth of units can be brought. The total points for all of the units in an army must be less than or equal to the Battle Cap, to ensure that strategy makes the decision for who wins the battle, not army selection. There will be common amounts, such as 1000 or 2000 points, which will have their own individual ratings board. Custom point caps will also exist so that players can make a battle of any size.
